Goose

From Goosepedia, the free encyclopedia
[Goose Image Placeholder]
Goose
Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Aves
Order: Anseriformes
Family: Anatidae

A goose (plural geese) is a bird of any of several waterfowl species in the family Anatidae. This group comprises the genera Anser (the grey geese and white geese) and Branta (the black geese). Some other birds, mostly related to the shelducks, have "goose" as part of their names.

Geese are distinct from ducks and swans, generally being intermediate in size. They are highly social animals and are well adapted for life on land and water.

Description

Geese are characterized by their long necks, webbed feet, and non-iridescent coloration. Both sexes usually look alike, although males (ganders) are often larger than females (dames). They have strong legs set further forward than those of ducks, which allows them to walk on land with ease.

Behavior

Social Structure

Geese are monogamous, living in permanent pairs throughout the year; however, unlike most other permanently monogamous animals, they are territorial only during the short nesting season. Paired geese are more dominant and feed more, two factors that result in more young.

Migration

Many geese migrate in large flocks. They fly in a distinctive V-formation, which reduces wind resistance for the birds flying behind the leader. This formation also facilitates communication and coordination within the flock.

Communication

Geese are vocal birds. They use a variety of calls, including honks, hisses, and cackles, to communicate with each other. Honking is often used to coordinate flight or warn of danger.

Relationship with humans

Domestication

Geese were one of the first animals to be domesticated. The domestic goose is derived from the greylag goose in Europe and the swan goose in Asia. They are kept for their meat, eggs, and down feathers.

Feeding in Parks

Many people enjoy feeding bread to geese in parks, although this is not recommended as it provides low nutritional value and can lead to health problems like "angel wing". Healthier alternatives include oats, seeds, and special pellet food.

Etymology

The word "geese" comes from the Proto-Indo-European *ghans-. In Germanic languages, the root word gave Old English gōs with the plural gēs.
